Questions to Ask

What are my key responsibilities? Who do I report to for different issues? What are the safety protocols? What tools/software will I use? What are the shift timings and break rules?

📝

Learning Notes

Carry a dedicated notebook. Note machine names, process flows, people's names and roles, safety rules, and any abbreviations you hear. Ask for process documents.

🤝

First Impression

Dress professionally, be punctual, show enthusiasm, listen more than you speak, take initiative to introduce yourself, and show respect to everyone regardless of position.

First Week Plan

Your roadmap for the first 7 days

📅 Day 1 - Orientation & Safety

▼
  • Complete all HR formalities and documentation
  • Attend safety induction training
  • Get PPE kit and understand usage
  • Take plant tour with supervisor
  • Meet all team members personally
  • Set up your workspace and tools

🔍 Day 2 - Process Observation

▼
  • Observe production process from start to finish
  • Understand raw material flow
  • Note quality checkpoints
  • Identify key machines and their functions
  • Ask operators about common issues

⚙️ Day 3 - Machine Understanding

▼
  • Study machine manuals and specifications
  • Understand machine controls and parameters
  • Learn about machine capabilities and limitations
  • Observe machine setup and changeover
  • Note maintenance schedules

📊 Day 4 - Documentation

▼
  • Learn about production reports and formats
  • Understand data collection methods
  • Study quality records and traceability
  • Review SOPs and work instructions
  • Practice filling out daily reports

🔧 Day 5 - Maintenance Observation

▼
  • Shadow maintenance team for a shift
  • Understand preventive maintenance schedule
  • Learn about common breakdowns and fixes
  • Study lubrication charts and schedules
  • Observe spare parts management

✅ Day 6 - Quality Observation

▼
  • Understand quality standards and specifications
  • Learn inspection methods and tools
  • Study rejection criteria and rework process
  • Observe quality audits if happening
  • Review quality documentation

🎯 Day 7 - Week Review

▼
  • Compile all notes from the week
  • Create a process flow diagram
  • List all machines with key parameters
  • Identify areas for improvement
  • Prepare questions for next week
  • Write weekly report for manager

First Month Plan

30-day acceleration program

📚

Technical Learning

Deep dive into your assigned machines and processes. Study engineering drawings, understand tolerances, learn about material specifications, and master the technical parameters of your area.

📝

Documentation

Maintain detailed daily logs. Document SOPs in your own words. Create visual aids and process maps. Build a personal knowledge base that will help you and future trainees.

📊

Daily Reporting

Master the art of daily reporting. Track production numbers, quality metrics, downtime reasons, and improvement suggestions. Make your reports data-driven and actionable.

📈

Excel Mastery

Learn advanced Excel for production tracking. Master pivot tables, charts, conditional formatting, VLOOKUP, and basic macros. Create dashboards for your area.

📐

Engineering Drawings

Practice reading and interpreting engineering drawings daily. Understand GD&T symbols, tolerances, fits, and surface finish requirements relevant to your products.

⚙️

Machine Components

Learn every component of your machines. Understand bearings, gears, shafts, couplings, belts, and chains. Know when each component needs replacement.

🏭

Shop Floor Behaviour

Build respect on the shop floor. Listen to operators, acknowledge their experience, ask thoughtful questions, and never act superior. Safety always first.

💬

Communication

Practice clear and concise communication. Report issues promptly, ask for help when needed, and keep your manager updated on progress and challenges.

🎯

Assignments

Complete all training assignments with excellence. Go beyond requirements. Show initiative by suggesting improvements based on your observations.

Maintenance Engineering

Keep machines running at peak performance

📅

Preventive Maintenance

Scheduled maintenance to prevent failures. Includes routine inspections, lubrication, cleaning, and component replacement based on time or usage intervals.

🔮

Predictive Maintenance

Condition-based monitoring using vibration, temperature, oil analysis, and ultrasound. Predict failures before they happen and schedule repairs optimally.

🚨

Breakdown Maintenance

Reactive maintenance when equipment fails. Focus on rapid response, root cause analysis, and implementing permanent fixes to prevent recurrence.

💧

Lubrication

Proper lubrication is the lifeblood of machinery. Understand oil types, grease selection, lubrication intervals, and contamination control.

📊

Condition Monitoring

Monitor equipment health through vibration analysis, thermography, oil analysis, and motor current signature analysis. Trend data over time.

📳

Vibration Analysis

Detect mechanical faults through vibration signatures. Identify imbalance, misalignment, bearing defects, and gear problems using FFT analysis.

🌳

Root Cause Analysis

Systematic problem-solving for maintenance failures. Use 5 Why, Fishbone, FMEA, and fault tree analysis to find and eliminate root causes.

📏

Machine Alignment

Precision alignment of shafts, couplings, and machine bases. Use dial indicators, laser alignment tools, and shimming techniques.

⚙️

Bearing Failure

Understand bearing failure modes: fatigue, corrosion, contamination, and misalignment. Learn to diagnose failures and select correct replacements.

🔌

Motor Maintenance

Electric motor care: insulation testing, bearing maintenance, alignment, cooling system checks, and rewinding decisions.

💧

Pump Maintenance

Centrifugal and positive displacement pump maintenance. Seal replacement, impeller inspection, alignment, and cavitation prevention.

💨

Compressor Maintenance

Air compressor care: filter changes, oil changes, belt tension, valve maintenance, and air quality monitoring.

Quality Engineering

Delivering excellence in every component

✅

Quality Control

Operational techniques to fulfill quality requirements. Includes inspection, testing, and measurement to ensure products meet specifications.

🛡️

Quality Assurance

Planned and systematic activities to provide confidence that quality requirements will be fulfilled. Focus on process improvement and prevention.

🔍

Inspection

Visual and dimensional inspection methods. First article inspection, in-process inspection, and final inspection procedures.

📐

Measurement Tools

Master all measurement instruments. Vernier caliper, micrometer, dial gauge, height gauge, bore gauge, and surface roughness tester.

📏

Vernier Caliper

Precision measurement tool with 0.02mm/0.001" least count. Learn to read vernier scale, zero error correction, and proper handling.

🔬

Micrometer

High-precision measurement with 0.01mm/0.0001" accuracy. Types: outside, inside, depth, and thread micrometers.

📊

Dial Gauge

Precision comparator instrument for measuring small deviations. Used for alignment, runout checking, and surface flatness.

📐

Height Gauge

Used for measuring vertical distances and marking. Vernier and digital height gauges with scriber attachment.

📐

GD&T

Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing. Understand datums, feature control frames, and all 14 geometric tolerances.

±

Tolerance

Understand dimensional tolerances, bilateral and unilateral tolerances, and tolerance stack-up analysis.

🔗

Fits

Clearance, interference, and transition fits. Hole basis and shaft basis systems. ISO tolerance grades and fit selection.

⚠️

FMEA

Failure Mode and Effects Analysis. Identify potential failures, assess risk priority numbers (RPN), and implement preventive actions.

📈

SPC

Statistical Process Control. Control charts (X-bar, R, p, c), process capability (Cp, Cpk), and process stability analysis.

📊

Pareto Analysis

80/20 rule application in quality. Identify vital few causes from trivial many. Prioritize improvement efforts effectively.

🐟

Fishbone Diagram

Ishikawa diagram for cause-and-effect analysis. Categories: Man, Machine, Material, Method, Measurement, Environment.

❓

5 Why

Iterative questioning technique to explore cause-and-effect relationships. Dig deeper until root cause is found.

📝

CAPA

Corrective and Preventive Action system. Document problems, implement fixes, verify effectiveness, and prevent recurrence.

📋

ISO Standards

ISO 9001 (Quality Management), ISO 14001 (Environment), ISO 45001 (Safety). Understand requirements and audit processes.

Safety

Safety first, always

🦺

PPE

Personal Protective Equipment: Safety helmets, safety shoes, safety glasses, ear protection, gloves, and respiratory protection. Always wear appropriate PPE for the task.

🔒

LOTO

Lock Out Tag Out procedures. Isolate energy sources before maintenance. Use proper locks, tags, and verification procedures. Never bypass LOTO.

🔥

Fire Safety

Know fire extinguisher types and locations. Understand fire classes (A, B, C, D, K). Know evacuation routes and assembly points. Participate in fire drills.

⚡

Electrical Safety

Understand electrical hazards and safe work practices. Use insulated tools, wear arc-rated PPE, and follow permit-to-work systems for electrical work.

☠️

Chemical Safety

Read SDS (Safety Data Sheets) for all chemicals. Understand hazard symbols, storage requirements, and emergency procedures for chemical spills.

🛡️

Machine Safety

Machine guarding requirements. Emergency stop buttons, interlocks, and safety light curtains. Never remove guards or bypass safety devices.

🚨

Emergency Response

Know emergency procedures for all scenarios. First aid basics, emergency contacts, and evacuation procedures. Be prepared, not scared.

⚠️

Industrial Hazards

Identify hazards: mechanical, chemical, electrical, thermal, and ergonomic. Use hierarchy of controls: Eliminate, Substitute, Engineer, Admin, PPE.

1 What is the difference between stress and strain?

▼
Stress is the internal resisting force per unit area when an external force is applied. Formula: σ = F/A (N/mm² or MPa).

Strain is the deformation per unit length. Formula: ε = ΔL/L (dimensionless).

Stress causes strain. Within the elastic limit, they are related by Hooke's Law: σ = E × ε, where E is Young's Modulus.

2 What is Young's Modulus?

▼
Young's Modulus (E) is the ratio of stress to strain within the elastic limit. It measures the stiffness of a material.

Formula: E = σ/ε = (F/A) / (ΔL/L)

Steel: ~200 GPa, Aluminum: ~70 GPa, Cast Iron: ~100-150 GPa. Higher E means stiffer material.

3 What is the difference between ductile and brittle materials?

▼
Ductile materials undergo significant plastic deformation before fracture. Examples: Mild steel, copper, aluminum. They show necking before breaking.

Brittle materials fracture with little or no plastic deformation. Examples: Cast iron, glass, ceramics. They fail suddenly without warning.

Ductility is measured by % elongation and % reduction in area.

4 What is the difference between hardness and toughness?

▼
Hardness is resistance to indentation, scratching, or abrasion. Measured by Brinell, Rockwell, or Vickers tests.

Toughness is the ability to absorb energy and deform plastically before fracturing. Measured by Charpy or Izod impact tests.

A material can be hard but not tough (e.g., hardened steel), or tough but not hard (e.g., mild steel).

5 What is the difference between casting and forging?

▼
Casting: Molten metal poured into a mold cavity. Good for complex shapes, internal cavities, and large parts. Lower strength due to grain structure.

Forging: Metal shaped by compressive forces (hammering/pressing). Better grain flow, higher strength, and fatigue resistance. Limited to simpler shapes.

Forging is preferred for high-stress applications; casting for complex geometries.

6 What is CNC machining?

▼
CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machining uses computer-controlled machine tools to remove material from a workpiece.

Advantages: High precision, repeatability, complex geometries, reduced human error, and faster production.

Common CNC machines: Lathe, Milling (VMC/HMC), EDM, and Grinding machines. Programming uses G-code and M-code.

7 What is the difference between a lathe and a milling machine?

▼
Lathe: Workpiece rotates while cutting tool is stationary (except feed movement). Used for cylindrical parts: turning, facing, threading, knurling.

Milling Machine: Cutting tool rotates while workpiece is stationary (or moves in X/Y). Used for flat surfaces, slots, gears, and complex profiles.

Lathe = rotating workpiece. Mill = rotating cutter.

8 What is GD&T?

▼
GD&T (Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing) is a symbolic language used to define the geometry of parts precisely.

It controls: Form (flatness, straightness), Orientation (parallelism, perpendicularity, angularity), Location (position, concentricity, symmetry), Runout, and Profile.

Benefits: Clear communication, functional tolerancing, and reduced manufacturing costs.

9 What is the difference between preventive and predictive maintenance?

▼
Preventive Maintenance: Scheduled maintenance based on time or usage (e.g., every 1000 hours). Proactive but may do unnecessary work.

Predictive Maintenance: Condition-based monitoring using sensors and data analysis. Maintenance is done only when needed. More efficient but requires investment in monitoring technology.

Predictive maintenance reduces downtime and costs compared to preventive maintenance.

10 What is OEE?

▼
OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness) measures manufacturing productivity. Formula: OEE = Availability × Performance × Quality.

Availability = Run Time / Planned Production Time
Performance = (Ideal Cycle Time × Total Count) / Run Time
Quality = Good Count / Total Count

World-class OEE is 85%+. Most factories operate at 40-60%.

S1 What is LOTO and why is it important?

▼
LOTO (Lock Out Tag Out) is a safety procedure to ensure dangerous machines are properly shut off and not started before maintenance is completed.

Steps: Notify affected employees, shut down equipment, isolate energy sources, apply locks and tags, verify zero energy state, perform maintenance, remove locks and tags, and restart.

It prevents accidental startup that could cause serious injury or death. It is mandatory and non-negotiable.

S2 What are the types of fire extinguishers?

▼
Class A: Ordinary combustibles (wood, paper) - Water or Foam
Class B: Flammable liquids (oil, gasoline) - Foam, CO2, or Dry Chemical
Class C: Electrical fires - CO2 or Dry Chemical (non-conductive)
Class D: Combustible metals (magnesium) - Dry Powder
Class K: Cooking oils and fats - Wet Chemical

Always check the label before using. PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.
